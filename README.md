# Better Choice — UX Case Study

A calm, non-judgmental habit companion designed to support consistency without pressure, streaks, or guilt.

---

## 🌿 Overview

Better Choice explores an alternative approach to habit tracking.

Instead of using streaks, reminders, or reward systems to drive engagement, this project focuses on creating a gentle and supportive experience that respects the user’s pace.

The goal is to help users build habits in a way that feels sustainable, calm, and self-directed.

---

## 🎯 Problem

Many habit-tracking apps rely on:
- streaks that create pressure
- notifications that interrupt
- reward systems that reinforce dependency

These patterns can lead to guilt, anxiety, and disengagement—especially when users miss a day.

---

## 🧠 Insight

Consistency does not come from pressure — it comes from feeling safe to continue.

When users feel judged or penalized, they are more likely to disengage. A more sustainable approach removes pressure and allows users to return at their own pace.

---

## 💡 Solution

Better Choice removes pressure-based mechanics and replaces them with:

- simple habit tracking (no streaks)
- gentle, supportive language
- flexible progress without penalties

The experience focuses on:

> progress without pressure

---

## ✨ Key Design Decisions

- Removed streaks to eliminate pressure and fear of failure  
- Replaced rewards with quiet acknowledgment (“You showed up today”)  
- Designed an open-ended empty state to encourage personal starting points  
- Created a subtle “Done” interaction focused on reflection rather than completion
